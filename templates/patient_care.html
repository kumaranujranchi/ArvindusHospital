Admission Process

A step-by-step guide to the admission process at Arvindu Hospitals

Step 1: Doctor's Recommendation

Consult with our specialist who will evaluate your condition and recommend admission if necessary.

Step 2: Pre-Admission Formalities

Complete registration forms and submit necessary documents at the admission desk.

Step 3: Room Allocation

Choose your preferred accommodation based on availability and get escorted to your room.

Required Documents

  • Doctor's admission recommendation
  • Valid photo ID proof (Aadhar Card, Voter ID, Passport, etc.)
  • Insurance details (if applicable)
  • Previous medical records and reports (if any)

Billing Process

For Cash Patients:
  1. An initial deposit is required at the time of admission
  2. Interim bills are generated every 3-4 days for extended stays
  3. Final settlement is done at the time of discharge
  4. Detailed itemized bills are provided for all services rendered
For Insurance Patients:
  1. Insurance card and pre-authorization are required at admission
  2. Our insurance desk will coordinate with your insurance provider
  3. You'll need to pay for expenses not covered under your policy
  4. All necessary documentation for claims will be provided

Patient Rights & Responsibilities

Understanding your rights and responsibilities as a patient

Your Rights as a Patient

  • Right to Respectful Care: To receive considerate and respectful care regardless of age, gender, religion, or financial status.
  • Right to Information: To receive complete and accurate information about your diagnosis, treatment, and prognosis in understandable terms.
  • Right to Informed Consent: To be informed about the risks, benefits, and alternatives to any procedure or treatment before giving consent.
  • Right to Privacy: To expect confidentiality of all communications and records related to your care.
  • Right to Refuse Treatment: To refuse treatment to the extent permitted by law and to be informed of the medical consequences of such refusal.
  • Right to Advance Directives: To formulate advance directives and have healthcare providers comply with these directives.
  • Right to Access Medical Records: To access your medical records and request amendments if necessary.
  • Right to File Complaints: To voice grievances regarding your care without fear of discrimination or reprisal.

Your Responsibilities as a Patient

  • Provide Information: To provide accurate and complete information about your health, medications, and medical history.
  • Follow Instructions: To follow the treatment plan recommended by your healthcare provider and report any unexpected changes in your condition.
  • Ask Questions: To ask questions when you do not understand information or instructions.
  • Hospital Rules: To follow hospital rules and regulations concerning patient conduct.
  • Respect and Consideration: To be considerate of the rights of other patients and hospital personnel, and to respect the property of others and the hospital.
  • Financial Obligations: To fulfill financial obligations associated with your care promptly.
  • Appointment Commitments: To keep appointments and notify the hospital or clinic when unable to do so.
  • Advance Directives: To inform your doctor and family about your advance directives regarding treatment decisions.
